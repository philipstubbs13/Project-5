About (me)

What led you to a career in web development?

Ever since I was young, I have always been adventurous and bold in trying something new no matter how difficult it was to learn. As a kid, I liked challenging myself to explore new opportunities whether it be learning another language, learning how to play piano, trying out for the basketball team despite not being as tall as other players, or striving to be one of the best chess players at school. I always found different ways to solve problems whether it be figuring out how to solve a difficult problem in math class, how to be a couple moves ahead of my chess opponent, or how to be an efficient three point shooter on the basketball court. Exploration, determination, and strategic thinking have been and still are a big part of who I am. My passion to explore new territories and figure out complex problems is what sparked my interest in web development.

I earned my bachelor's degree in Scientific and Technical Communication at the University of Minnesota in 2013, which helped give me an understanding of front end development (mainly HTML and CSS), an understanding of web design best practices, and an understanding of how to write and design for the web. My passion for coding came when I decided to create my first website during my senior year. Creating the site was challenging, but that experience helped me uncover my passion.

After 4 years of working as a technical writer in software development, I decided to follow my dream of becoming a user experience developer. As a result, I enrolled in the University of Minnesota coding boot camp. Since enrolling, my passion for learning about technologies like CSS and Javascript continues to grow. My interest in how those technologies impact the design and user experience of the web is why I'm excited to continue learning more.

What role do you typically play on a team?

Growing up, I played a lot of team sports from baseball to soccer to basketball. So, ever since I was about five or six years old, I had to learn how to work and communicate with others on a team to succeed and achieve a common goal. Whether that goal was winning the championship game or finishing a big team project at work before the deadline, teamwork, communication, and everybody knowing his or her role were extremely important. For example, in basketball, the offense runs through the point guard. The point guard is expected to lead the team and call out the plays for the team to execute. Even though the leader of the team is really the coach, it is the point guard who helps the team work together and helps make plays happen. Without a point guard to help the coach, the team is not going to be very cohesive and successful.

As for myself, when I work with a team on a project, I do consider myself to be like a point guard on a basketball team. That is, I like to lead and manage the project, but I wouldn't consider myself a very outspoken leader like a basketball coach. My role in the group is often mediator. As a mediator, I am a silent, secondary leader on the team. That is, I don't necessarily act as the leader of the team, but I often help the team communicate with one another, make decisions, and help keep the project moving forward. So, just like a point guard is often a game manager, I often find myself on teams where I'm acting as a project manager to help organize and drive the team to reach their goal.

Whether I'm on the court or at work, I need to know that I can rely on my team and that they can rely on me. As a game manager when playing basketball or as a project manager at work, I want to make sure everyone knows where they are going and what they need to do.

How did you become interested in user experience?

I first became interested and exposed to user experience testing during my senior year at the University of Minnesota while I was in the Scientific and Technical Communication program. Specifically, during my senior year, I took a usability testing and human factors course. Throughout the course, the class was split up into teams, and each team was responsible for conducting a usability test for a part of the Hennepin County Library website at the University of Minnesota usability lab. As part of this project, the team I was working on completed a heuristic evaluation of the website to help determine usability strengths and weaknesses of the current site. We put together a usability test plan outlining the purpose of the test, the research question, the testing scenarios, and the methodology. We then conducted the test. Finally, we communicated our results and recommendations to the Hennepin County Library.

This experience was eye-opening for me. First of all, it helped me realized how important user-centered developoment and design of a website really is. It is very important to understand the user and keep the user at the forefront of every design decision. It also helped me understand my purpose as a web developer. That is, my purpose is to make users' lives more simple. An application can look cool and have all these interesting features. But, if a user can't actually accomplish anything with it, doesn't understand what they are supposed to be doing, or if the application is too difficult to use, they aren't going to use it and the app you just built is worthless. Improving the user experience of a site or application is why learned how to code. The user is why I'm passionate about learning and growing as a developer.
