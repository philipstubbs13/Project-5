About me

What led you to coding?

Ever since I was young, I was always adventurous and loved to learn. As a kid, I liked challenging myself to explore new opportunities whether it be learning another language, learning how to play piano, trying out for the basketball team despite not being as tall or athletic as other players, or striving to be one of the best chess players at school. I always found different ways to solve problems whether it be figuring out how to solve a difficult problem in math class, how to be a couple moves ahead of my chess opponent, or how to be an efficient three point shooter on the basketball court. Exploration, determination, and strategic thinking have been and still are a big part of who I am. My passion to explore, tinker, and figure things out on my own is what sparked my interest in web development.

I earned my bachelor's degree in Scientific and Technical Communication at the University of Minnesota in 2013, which helped give me an understanding of front end development (mainly HTML and CSS), an understanding of web design best practices, and an understanding of how to write and design for the web. My passion for coding came when I decided to create my first website during my senior year. Creating the site was challenging, but that experience helped me uncover my passion.

After 4 years of working as a technical writer in software development, I decided to follow my dream of developing and enroll in the University of Minnesota coding boot camp. Since enrolling, my passion for learning about technologies like CSS and Javascript continues to grow. My interest in how those technologies impact the design and user experience of the web is why I'm excited to continue learning about web development.
